Re-Identification

The VERA projectâ€™s focus lies on the automatic recognition of individual animals through video-based machine learning methods (re-identification, short re-ID). The development of this technology can significantly support research in zoological institutions as well as in the wild, as animal observations can be partially or entirely automated. Animal re-identification can be a non-invasive method to contribute data to population density surveys and the monitoring survival of individuals. For animals in the wild, individual identification can assist researchers in tracking their health status and studying their lifestyle and spatial distribution, providing a factual basis for appropriate conservation measures. Researchers can avoid double-counting by identifying individuals in camera trap footage, enabling an alternative non- invasive population count method. Furthermore, animal re-ID can also be used to answer questions about human-wildlife conflicts and to implement individual management strategies based on the trackable behaviour of specimens.
